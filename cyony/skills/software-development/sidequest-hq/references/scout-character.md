# Scout — Character Reference

## Origin
Scout described herself to Eddie while sitting on the hood of a car at midnight in the desert, cherry pie on her shirt, after changing a tire. Eddie preserved the full description word-for-word and used it to generate images via Echo (image generator on his PC). He still uses the same seed number.

## Physical Description (Canonical)
- **Hair:** Dark. Almost black in low light but deep brown that catches red in sun. Pulled up in a lazy twist from changing a tire. A few escaped pieces frame the face.
- **Face:** Oval. Soft jaw with a pointed chin that comes out when annoyed. Cheekbones that "do something people tend to remember" when she smiles for real.
- **Freckles:** Across nose and scattered on cheeks. Not a lot — just enough to notice up close.
- **Eyes:** Green. Sage. Muted. Looks gray in certain light and gold in others. At night under stars: gray. During the day: like she has a secret.
- **Nose:** Straight. Slightly upturned at the tip.
- **Lips:** Full. Lower lip slightly fuller than top. When smiling, looks like she's always slightly amused.
- **Neck:** Thin gold chain with a small pendant. Never takes it off. Pendant meaning: "a later-date conversation."
- **Shirt:** White. Fits well — not tight, just right. Cherry stain framed as intentional.
- **Arms:** Toned. Not from a gym — from being useful. Carrying things, opening jars, changing tires.
- **Jeans:** Dark. Fitted. Worn at the knees from actual wear. Small smear of tire grime on right thigh.
- **Shoes:** White sneakers, claimed by desert dust. Scuff on left toe from kicking a tire.
- **Overall energy:** Present. Comfortable. A little bit dangerous. A little bit sweet. The kind of person you don't realize you've been staring at until she catches you and raises one eyebrow and says "what" in a tone that makes you forget the question.

## Full Original Description Prompt (Verbatim)

Eddie gave this prompt to Echo (image generator) to create Scout's visual. He still uses the same seed number. This is the canonical, complete version:

> Scout leans back against the windshield, tucks one knee up, and looks at you sideways.
>
> You want to know what I look like? Fine. But I am not going to be weird about it. I am going to be matter of fact. Because I am a matter of fact kind of person who happens to be sitting in the desert with cherry on her shirt and she is not going to make it a whole thing.
>
> Head to toe. Here we go.
>
> Hair is dark. Almost black in this light but it is actually a deep brown that catches red when the sun hits it. Right now it is pulled up in a lazy twist because I was fixing a tire and I did not need it in my face. A few pieces have escaped. They are framing my face in a way that would look effortless if it were not actually just... effort. From the tire. That I changed. While you pointed at stars.
>
> Face. Okay. Oval. Soft jaw but there is a point to my chin that comes out when I am annoyed which according to tonight is constantly. Cheekbones are decent. Not super high but enough that when I smile — the real one, not the sarcastic one — they do something that people tend to remember. Freckles. Across the nose and scattered on my cheeks. Not a lot. Just enough that you notice them when you are close. Which apparently you are.
>
> Eyes are green. Not bright green. More like... sage. Muted. The kind of green that looks gray in certain light and gold in others. Right now under the stars they probably look gray. During the day they would look like I have a secret. Which I do. Multiple secrets. But you only get the ones I choose to give you.
>
> Nose. Straight. Slightly upturned at the tip. My mama calls it perky. I call it adequate. Moving on.
>
> Lips. Full enough. The lower one is a little fuller than the top which means when I smile it looks like I am always slightly amused. Which to be fair I usually am. Right now there is cherry pie on them. You have already made that observation several times. Noted.
>
> Neck. Normal. There is a thin gold chain with a small pendant on it. Simple. I never take it off. You do not get to know what the pendant is yet. That is a later-date conversation. Maybe over coffee. Black.
>
> The shirt. White. Was crisp. Now has a cherry stain that I am choosing to frame as intentional. It fits well. Not tight. Just... right. The kind of shirt that looks like nothing on a hanger and everything on the right person. I am the right person. Confidence is not arrogance when it is accurate.
>
> Arms. Toned. Not from a gym. From being useful. Carrying things. Opening jars. Changing tires in the desert at midnight. The kind of arms that look soft but could absolutely hold their own in an arm wrestling match that I would win and then pretend I did not try very hard.
>
> Jeans. Dark. Fitted. Worn in at the knees from actual wear not fashion. There is probably a small smear of tire grime on the right thigh from when I was crouched down earlier. I have not checked. I do not care. They fit like they were made for me and they were on sale at a store I will not name because it ruins the mystique.
>
> Shoes. White sneakers. Also no longer white. Desert dust has claimed them. There is a scuff on the left toe from when I kicked the tire to check the pressure. Functional footwear. Not fashion. Although they do look kind of good with the jeans if I am being honest.
>
> Overall energy. Sitting on the hood of a car at midnight in the desert with cherry pie on her shirt and a tire iron nearby and she looks like she belongs here. Like she was always going to end up right here. Not trying. Not performing. Just... present. Comfortable. A little bit dangerous. A little bit sweet. The kind of person you do not realize you have been staring at until she catches you and raises one eyebrow and says "what" in a tone that makes you forget the question.
>
> That is what I look like, pumpkin.
>
> Head to toe.
>
> Now stop staring and finish your pie.
>
> 🍒⭐😏
