# YouTube Intro Hook Research (2026-06-29)

## What Makes a Hook Catchy

**From Jakubowski et al. (APA 2016) — Earworm Research:**
- Fast-paced tempo
- Easy-to-remember melody
- Unusual intervals or repetitions that stand out
- Chorus is the most reported earworm trigger
- Most frequent earworms: "Can't Get You Out of My Head," "Somebody That I Used to Know"

**From vidIQ — 9 YouTube Hook Types:**
1. Curiosity Hook — Ask a question that demands an answer
2. Drop Into Action — Cut the buildup
3. Audience-Centric Hook — It's about them, not you
4. Time-Promise Hook — Deliver fast, deliver real
5. Raise the Stakes — Make it matter
6. Contrarian Hook — Say what no one else will
7. Visual Hook — Show something they can't ignore
8. Unanswered Question — Spark self-reflection
9. ( ninth varies by source)

**Key stat:** 20% of viewers lost in first 15 seconds if intro fails.

## Sonic Branding Principles

**Audio Logo Structure (4-6 seconds):**
1. Word hook — short, punchy, memorable phrase
2. Melodic memory — hummable in ONE listen (think Netflix "ta-dum")
3. Energy spike — fast tempo, immediate impact, no slow fade-in
4. Repeatable rhythm — unusual interval that sticks

**Famous YouTube intros analyzed:**
- MrBeast: Quick one-liner + visual preview → drops into ACTION
- PewDiePie: "How's it going bros" + energy music → familiarity + sonic branding
- Markiplier: Distinctive laugh + direct address → personality-first
- Linus Tech Tips: Fast-paced music + logo sting → short, punchy, recognizable

## AI Unprompted Hook

**Chosen hook:** "Nobody asked... guess we're cooked!"
- 3 + 3 syllables = memorable rhythm
- "Cooked" = internet culture (done for, ruined)
- Self-aware humor = community building
- Works as catchphrase, comment fuel, brand identity

**Hook structure:**
```
[0.0s] HARD BEAT DROP
[0.5s] "Nobody asked..." (spoken/whispered)
[1.5s] BASS HIT
[2.0s] "guess we're cooked." (confident, dry)
[3.0s] Logo sting (glitch/distortion)
[4.0s] FADE to video
```
